What I learn From DEFCON

By Jiashu Wang

  In the summer of my second year of college, I went to Las Vegas with my friends to the DEFCON. For the people who don't know what DEFCON is, it is a security conference that happens in Vegas every year.
  I went there to meet up with my friends on the first day and stayed in an airb&b with each other.
  This events hold every year, so if any person would want to go, there are still chance to attend the conference.

  Inside the DEFCON, there are different areas which we call camps. For instance the IOT camp is a really hot spot that everyone would love to go.
  Personally, it was my first time and I just tried to go around and attend most of the camps.
  First thing that we have to talk about is the badges. Every year the badge would be different. And a lot of times, the badge would be a computer chip that you can play around with.
  In every single camp, there are always classes, competitions or talks that you can attend and learn. It is always a good learning experances for any one to attend.
  I, Personally, learned a lot about different things such as how to save yourself from a handcuff. Or how to create a honey pot, which is a fake commend line system to trick the people who hacked into your computer.

  In conclusion, it is very valuable for me to went to one, and learn so many things in the convension.
  I wish I can go for another day.
